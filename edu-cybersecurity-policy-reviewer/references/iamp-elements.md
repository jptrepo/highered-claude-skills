# Information Assurance Management Plan (IAMP) Elements
Derived from: NSF Research Infrastructure Guide §5.3 and Corn "Policy by Design" framework

An IAMP is a *living disclosure document* — not a policy itself, but a structured articulation of how
an institution has chosen to govern and operate its information assurance program. It should require
senior leadership sign-off and serve as a vehicle for resource conversations.

---

## Core IAMP Principles

- **NOT prescriptive by policy** — the policy mandates an IAMP exists; the IAMP itself captures the institution's choices
- **Living document** — designed to evolve; should have a defined review/revision cadence
- **Senior management accountability** — requires sign-off at the executive level (CIO, Provost, or equivalent)
- **Framework-neutral at policy level** — the IAMP names the framework(s); the policy does not

---

## Required IAMP Elements

### 1. Governance & Authority
- Identity and title of designated decision maker (CISO or equivalent)
- Reporting structure and institutional position
- Decision taxonomy: what authority is held ex ante vs. requires escalation
- Governance body name, charter summary, and meeting cadence
- Escalation pathways for cross-functional incidents

### 2. Framework Alignment
- Named security framework(s) the institution aligns to (e.g., NIST CSF, ISO 27001, CIS Controls)
- Rationale for framework selection
- Maturity level or tier currently operating at
- Gap assessment cadence

### 3. Security Functions Inventory
- Enumeration of active security capabilities/functions currently in place
- Gaps or planned additions
- Ownership of each function (team or role responsible)

### 4. Roles and Responsibilities
- CISO / security leadership
- IT operations interfaces
- Data owners and stewards
- Research computing / research security roles
- End-user responsibilities summary

### 5. Multi-Mission Environment Mapping
- Enterprise/administrative systems profile
- Research environment profile (CUI, export control, regulated data)
- Student-facing systems profile
- Any federated or decentralized IT units and their relationship to central security

### 6. Risk Management Approach
- Risk assessment methodology in use
- Risk tolerance statement (aligned to institutional mission)
- How risk decisions are documented and communicated

### 7. Security Practice Registry Reference
- Reference to the CISO-maintained security practice registry
- Process by which practices are added, modified, or removed
- Communication mechanism for practice changes to affected stakeholders

### 8. Compliance Obligations Inventory
- Applicable regulatory frameworks (HIPAA, FERPA, CMMC, FISMA, state laws, etc.)
- Research-specific requirements (NSPM-33, NIST SP 800-171, etc.)
- Audit schedule and responsible parties

### 9. Incident Response Summary
- Incident classification taxonomy
- Notification requirements and timelines
- Cross-functional coordination contacts (legal, communications, HR, facilities)

### 10. Metrics & Program Completeness
- How program health is measured (avoid pseudo-scientific risk scores)
- Preferred: program completeness metrics and outcome metrics
- Reporting cadence to governance body and executive leadership
- Evidence generation: how program execution produces its own audit trail

### 11. Resourcing Summary
- Current staffing model
- Budget allocation mechanism
- Known resource gaps and advocacy status
- Governance committee role in resource advocacy

### 12. Review & Revision
- Designated IAMP owner
- Review trigger events (major incident, significant threat landscape change, leadership change)
- Scheduled review cadence (recommended: annual minimum)
- Version control and approval workflow

---

## IAMP vs. Policy: Key Distinctions

| Policy Says | IAMP Says |
|-------------|-----------|
| A CISO shall be designated | The CISO is [Name], reporting to [Title] |
| A security framework shall be adopted | We align to NIST CSF at Tier 3 |
| An IAMP shall be maintained | [This document] |
| A security practice registry shall exist | Registry is located at [location], maintained by [role] |
| Governance shall advocate for resources | Current resource gaps and advocacy status: [details] |

---

## Notes for Higher Education Context

- Research universities may need **separate IAMP annexes** for research computing environments, 
  especially those handling CUI or operating under CMMC requirements
- Decentralized IT governance (common in R1s) requires explicit language about how 
  distributed units interface with the central IAMP
- Faculty governance concerns: IAMP should acknowledge monitoring/logging practices and 
  their relationship to academic freedom policies
